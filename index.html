Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar



  • Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar, born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow, India, overcame significant social discrimination to excel academically, earning degrees from Columbia University and the London School of Economics.

  • He was a staunch advocate for Dalit rights and fought against untouchability, playing a pivotal role in drafting the Indian Constitution, which enshrined fundamental rights and equality for all citizens.

  • In 1956, he led a mass conversion to Buddhism to reject the Hindu caste system and seek social justice. Celebrated as the "Father of the Indian Constitution," Ambedkar contributions continue to inspire social reform movements.

  • He passed away on December 6, 1956, and is honored through numerous memorials and institutions, with his writings and reforms having a lasting impact on Indian society.

  • His role in drafting the Indian Constitution not only laid the foundation for a democratic and inclusive India but also redefined the rights and roles of marginalized communities.

  • Ambedkar was deeply influenced by Western philosophy and liberal democratic ideals. His studies at Columbia University and the London School of Economics exposed him to various political and economic theories, which shaped his vision for social reform in India.

  • Ambedkar was instrumental in establishing several educational institutions for marginalized communities. Notable among these are the Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University in Aurangabad and the Ambedkar Institute of Technology in Bangalore.

  • Ambedkar envisioned a society where the principles of justice, equality, and fraternity would be central. His vision extended beyond legal and political reforms to include economic and social dimensions, aiming for a comprehensive transformation of Indian society.

  • Ambedkar authored several important works, including “Annihilation of Caste,” which criticizes the Hindu caste system and calls for its labolition. His writings continue to be studied and revered for their insight into social and political issues.

  • In 1932, Ambedkar negotiated the Poona Pact with Mahatma Gandhi, which led to the allocation of reserved seats for Dalits in legislative bodies while addressing Gandhi’s concerns about separate electorates.
